About Me

I am a 3rd year Ph.D. student in the Dept. of Earth, Planetary, and Space Sciences at UCLA currently working with Prof. David Paige on the NASA LRO Diviner team studying the thermal and spectral properties of the Lunar and Martian surface. My primary interest is in the application of statistics and machine learning to (1) planetary surfaces, (2) their evolution, and (3) implications for the distribution of ices.

As a recent intern at the Jet Propulsion Laboratory, I worked with the Mars Science team to map the extent of Martian south polar ice sublimation features. Prior to this, I worked as a Staff Engineer at Booz Allen Hamilton, providing geoscience and machine learning support to their Strategic Innovation Group (SIG) and Aerospace & GPS accounts.

My undergraduate degree is in Statistics with a minor in Geophysics and Space Physics from UCLA.
