About Me

I am a 3rd year Ph.D. student in the Dept. of Earth, Planetary, and Space Sciences at UCLA. I currently work with Prof. David Paige on the NASA LRO Diviner team studying the thermal and spectral properties of the Lunar surface. I am interested in the application of statistics and machine learning to planetary surfaces, their evolution, and implications for the distribution of ices.

I recently worked with Dr. Serina Diniega and Dr. Lauren Mc Keown at the Jet Propulsion Laboratory to map the extent of Martian south polar ice sublimation features. Prior to this I worked as a Staff Engineer at Booz Allen Hamilton, providing geoscience and machine learning support to their Strategic Innovation Group (SIG) and Aerospace & GPS accounts.

My undergraduate degree is in Statistics with a minor in Geophysics and Space Physics from UCLA.
